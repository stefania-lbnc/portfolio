blindex 2026 Full Brand identity | Commission Brand Identity blindex S.P.A. Graphic Designer Logo, Visual Identity, Brand Guidelines How do you make a technical product feel like a design choice? Blindex makes custom roller blinds for offices, hotels, and commercial spaces. The client came to me needing a full brand identity — logo, guidelines, the works.
The real challenge wasn't the aesthetics. It was figuring out how to communicate something functional and technical in a way that felt refined and considered — without losing sight of what the product actually does.

The brief said "elegant and professional." I kept asking: what does elegant actually look like for a blind that disappears into a ceiling? Starting from the product, not the mood board I built the concept around what the product actually does: it moves. Smoothly, quietly, always controlled. That gesture — a roller blind in motion — became the foundation for everything: the logo, the tone, the spacing, the palette.
I called it Dynamic Flow. Not as a tagline, but as a design principle to keep every decision anchored to something real.

One letter does all the work

I wanted a wordmark with a handcrafted feel — soft curves, continuous stroke, nothing rigid. The letterforms needed to communicate quality without looking like every other sans-serif identity out there.
The "e" became the key. Its shape naturally echoes the scrolling motion of a blind — fluid, controlled, never breaking. Giving it a different colour wasn't just a stylistic choice: it's where the whole concept lives, visible at any size.
The rest of the wordmark stays grounded so the "e" can move.

I didn't want a cold, corporate palette. Blindex works with architects and interior designers — people who care about how a space feels, not just how it looks on paper.
Deep Black and Ardesia Red anchor the primary system. The secondaries — Smoke, Pearl, Sage, Sand — give the brand room to breathe across different contexts without losing coherence. Sand in particular became the default background: warm, quiet, never competing with the content.

Two fonts, one direction

Comfortaa for titles — geometric and soft, it carries the same warmth as the palette without being decorative. It's the face you see first and it sets the tone immediately.
Source Sans Pro for everything else — clean, highly legible, completely unobtrusive. It lets the content speak without getting in the way.
Together they cover the full range: from a bold heading on a presentation to a small caption on an installation manual.

brand in use

The brand book covers all the touchpoints a B2B brand actually needs: stationery, digital presentations, signage, environmental applications. The goal was a system flexible enough to work across all of them without needing to reinvent anything each time.
If the guidelines are clear, the identity takes care of itself.
