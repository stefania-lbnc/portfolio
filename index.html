background

I studied Industrial Design at La Sapienza University in Rome, and somewhere between typography and layout systems I realised this was exactly where I wanted to be.

Since then, I've been working across editorial, advertising, and digital — always trying to find that balance between visual clarity and something that makes you look twice. One of my recent projects was the full redesign of the Egyptian Academy's magazine in Rome: a chance to bring a fresh, contemporary identity to an institution with a lot of history.

I hold a degree in Industrial Design from La Sapienza University in Rome and enjoy exploring different design challenges, always aiming for simple, effective solutions.

how i work

I'd call my approach experimental — not in the sense of throwing things at the wall, but in the sense that I like to question the expected solution before committing to it.

I'm especially drawn to UX/UI design right now, where visual structure and usability overlap. Getting both right — so a design feels intuitive and still has personality — is the kind of challenge I love.

I work with Figma, Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, and Photoshop. I'm comfortable working independently or as part of a creative team

what i do | how i do it
Identity Design

A logo should work in one colour, at any size, and still feel like it means something. I focus on marks that are distinctive without trying too hard.

Editorial Design

Layouts are invisible when they work — you just read. I design for rhythm and hierarchy, so the content always leads.

UX/UI Design

Where structure meets feeling. I'm drawn to interfaces that guide you without you noticing — intuitive, not sterile..

Promotional Graphics

Visual communication that earns attention without shouting. Simple ideas, executed well — that's what sticks.
